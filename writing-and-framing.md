# Good Writing & Framing Templates

> Messaging patterns and templates extracted from top-tier AI/agent product marketing (Notion, and proven GTM strategies).

---

## Core Framing Principles

### 1. Busywork vs. Life's Work
**The most powerful emotional hook in B2B AI messaging.**

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Prefer |
|----------|-----------|
| "Automate your tasks" | "Spend less time on busywork, more on life's work" |
| "Save time on repetitive tasks" | "From busywork to life's work" |
| "Be more productive" | "Do the work only you can do" |

**Why it works:** Connects to a deeper emotional truth — people don't want more productivity, they want to escape tedium and do meaningful work.

**Source:** Notion's entire 3.0/3.3 launch messaging hinges on this contrast.

---

### 2. AI as Teammates, Not Tools
**Shift from "it helps you" to "it works with you."**

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Prefer |
|----------|-----------|
| "AI assistant" | "AI teammate" |
| "AI that helps you" | "AI that works while you sleep" |
| "Your AI helper" | "Autonomous teammate that runs 24/7" |

**Why it works:** Teammates are proactive, ongoing, and part of the team. Tools are on-demand and transactional.

**Source:** Notion Custom Agents — "Your Agent" → "AI teammates"

---

### 3. Specific Outcomes Over General Claims
**Never say "improve productivity" without a number.**

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Prefer |
|----------|-----------|
| "Save time" | "Save 20 hours/week" |
| "Work faster" | "From 6 hours to 10 minutes" |
| "Be more efficient" | "Handle 25%+ of tickets autonomously" |

**Why it works:** Vague promises feel like marketing. Specific outcomes feel like proof.

**Source:** Ramp: "300+ agents", Remote: "20 hours/week saved", Braintrust: "20 minutes/day"

---

### 4. Context-Aware Positioning
**Emphasize that AI understands YOUR work, not generic prompts.**

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Prefer |
|----------|-----------|
| "AI that answers questions" | "AI that knows YOUR knowledge base" |
| "Write better copy" | "Answers questions using knowledge you already have" |
| "Generate content" | "Pulls from your docs, databases, and tools" |

**Why it works:** Generic AI feels like a stranger. Context-aware AI feels like it actually knows your business.

**Source:** Notion: "Uses your existing docs and databases as context"

---

### 5. The Reactive → Proactive Flip
**Position AI as something that works WITHOUT being asked.**

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Prefer |
|----------|-----------|
| "Ask your AI anything" | "Set a trigger, let it run" |
| "Get answers on demand" | "Works whether you're online or not" |
| "When you need it, just ask" | "Runs on autopilot" |

**Why it works:** Most AI still requires prompting. Autonomous feels revolutionary.

**Source:** Notion Custom Agents: "No manual prompting needed after setup"

## Template Library

### A. Product Announcement — Agent Launch

**Structure:**
1. TL;DR (3-5 bullet points, leading with the value)
2. The problem (busywork reality)
3. The solution (what the product does)
4. Proof points (specific outcomes)
5. Call to action

**Example:**
```
TL;DR:
- [Product] is now available
- It handles [recurring work] 24/7
- [Company] saved [X hours/week] using it
- No code required — just describe what you need

The problem:
It's 3 p.m. on a Thursday. You have follow-ups to write, the team is 
waiting for [deliverable], and you're still hunting through [sources] 
for that one detail from last week. Then suddenly, the day is over.

The solution:
[Product] is an autonomous [teammate/agent] that [what it does]. 
Anything you can do manually, your [agent] does for you — automatically, 
in the background, whether you're online or not.

What teams are actually building:
- [Use case 1]: [specific example with metric]
- [Use case 2]: [specific example with metric]

Try it → [CTA]
```

**Source patterns:** Notion 3.0, Notion 3.3 Custom Agents launches

---

### B. Feature Highlight — Automation Capability

**Structure:**
1. One-line summary (what it does)
2. The before/after (manual vs. automated)
3. Specific workflow example
4. Outcome metric

**Example:**
```
Auto-route incoming requests to the right people — no manual triage needed.

Before: [Person] hunts for the right channel, copies the details, 
@mentions the right owner, and manually creates a task. 15 minutes 
per request. Often loses context in transit.

After: Agent watches for new requests in [source], automatically 
categorizes by [criteria], enriches with [context], creates a task 
in [tool], and notifies the right owner. Done in seconds.

Real result: Remote's IT team saved 20 hours/week with 95%+ triage 
accuracy. 25%+ of tickets resolved autonomously.
```

**Source patterns:** Notion "How Notion uses Custom Agents" blog

### C. Case Study / Social Proof

**Structure:**
1. Company/team name (if permission allowed)
2. The pain point (what was tedious)
3. The solution (agent setup)
4. The outcome (specific, quantified)
5. Quote (optional, adds humanity)

**Example:**
```
Ramp's internal AI team built over 300 Custom Agents. Their 
Product Oracle answers dozens of questions every day about the 
product roadmap and how features work — questions that previously 
required a product manager to respond manually.

"We used to monitor those channels constantly. Now our agents 
answer questions automatically, and we just audit responses and 
feed improvements back into the Custom Agent." — Ben Levick, 
Head of Operations and Internal AI, Ramp
```

**Source patterns:** Notion Customer Stories, release notes

### E. Competitive Positioning

**Structure:**
1. What it's NOT (clarify the category)
2. What it IS (differentiate)
3. Why it matters (outcome)

**Example:**
```
This isn't another AI chatbot that makes generic suggestions. 
Your [Agent] tackles real work because it understands your 
business context. It can complete multiple actions at a time, 
creating finished pages, databases, and reports directly in 
your workspace.

Unlike traditional automation tools, [Product] combines:
- Deep integration with your knowledge base
- Natural-language setup (no code)
- Shared team access with enterprise-grade controls
- Multi-model AI support (Claude, GPT, Gemini)
```

**Source patterns:** Notion "Notion 3.0 is here" blog

### F. Activation / Onboarding Prompt

**Structure:**
1. Hook (the recurring pain)
2. Solution (agent can handle it)
3. Example (specific task)
4. Encouragement (low friction)

**Example:**
```
That task you do every [Friday/Monday/morning] — the one that 
takes [X] minutes but doesn't really need your brain?

Your Custom Agent can handle it.

Example: "Every Friday at 4pm, query the [database] for tasks 
with [Status = Done] and [Due Date = this week], group by 
[Assignee], and post a formatted summary to #team-updates."

The more specific your instructions, the more reliable the 
results. Start with one recurring task. See what happens.
```

**Source patterns:** Notion Help Center, best practices guides

```
- Take actions: post reports, create tasks, update records, send messages

Set them up once — building a workflow for repetitive tasks like 
weekly reports or triaging feedback — and it becomes a shared resource 
the whole team relies on.

Start → [Link to template or guide]
```

```
[Name] answers questions so your team doesn't have to.
```

```
Incoming [requests/tickets/emails] → routed to the right person → done.
```

```
of custom agents teams have built in the first [weeks/months].
```

### L. Migration / Transformation Narrative

Notion frames the shift to agents as a fundamental workflow change.

**"Move 100% Away" Template:**
```
We're moving 100% away from managing work directly in [tools] 
and moving to only chatting with [Agents].

Instead of [opening the database → filtering for X → updating Y manually], 
you just tell your agent what you need. It handles the rest.

[Company] is [X]% of the way there. Their team doesn't manage work in 
[database] anymore — they manage it through conversation with agents.
```

**"From Tool User to Team Lead" Template:**
```
You used to be the one doing the work. Now you're the one directing it.

Instead of: open tool → find info → manually process → update records
You do: tell agent what you need → review result → done

Your role shifts from worker to team lead — directing your AI teammates 
who handle the execution.
```

## Using These Additional Templates

1. **Match agent type to template** — Q&A, routing, status updates, or custom build
2. **Lead with "24/7" or "while you sleep"** for any autonomous positioning
3. **Include adaptation language** — "learns your style," "your permissions"
4. **Emphasize "no rebuild"** when targeting teams with existing knowledge bases
5. **Use migration framing** ("100% away from") for transformational use cases
