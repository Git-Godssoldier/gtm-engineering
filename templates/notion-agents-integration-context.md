# Notion Agents — Integration & Context Positioning

> Templates for positioning AI agents with emphasis on existing tools and knowledge, extracted from notion.com/product/agents (2026)

---

## Integration-First Framing

Notion emphasizes "no rebuild required" — agents use what you already have.

### Template
```
Works where your team already works.

[Product] uses your existing [docs / databases / tools] as context. 
It runs inside [primary workspace] and connects across [integrations]. 
No rebuilding knowledge. No switching apps.

Your agents see what your team sees. They work with the same data. 
They fit into your existing workflows.
```

### Example
```
Notion Agents use your existing docs and databases as context. 
They run inside Notion and connect across Slack, Mail, Calendar, 
Figma, Linear, and more! No rebuilding knowledge, no switching apps.
```

### "No Switching Apps" Variant
```
Don't rebuild. Don't switch. Just connect.

[Product] connects across [Slack, Mail, Calendar, Figma, Linear, 
and custom MCP servers]. Your knowledge lives where it already lives. 
Your agents just know how to find it.

No migration. No new systems to learn. Just better automation on top 
of what you have.
```

## Context-Aware Positioning

Emphasize that AI understands YOUR work, not generic prompts.

### Template
```
Your agent works while you sleep — but it knows what you know.

It pulls from your existing docs, databases, and connected tools. So when 
it handles [task], it's using [your knowledge / your context / your data] 
to do it right.

No rebuilding knowledge. No starting from scratch. Your agent inherits 
everything you already have.
```

### Example
```
Notion Agents use your existing docs and databases as context. 
They know what your team knows. They work with your data, your 
knowledge, your tools.

When it answers a question, it's pulling from YOUR knowledge base.
When it writes a report, it's using YOUR databases.
When it routes a task, it sees what YOUR team sees.
```

### Comparison Table

| ❌ Avoid | ✅ Prefer |
|----------|-----------|
| "AI that answers questions" | "AI that knows YOUR knowledge base" |
| "Write better copy" | "Answers questions using knowledge you already have" |
| "Generate content" | "Pulls from your docs, databases, and tools" |
| "General-purpose AI" | "AI that understands YOUR business context" |

## Migration / Transformation Narrative

Frame the shift to agents as a fundamental workflow change.

### "Move 100% Away" Template
```
We're moving 100% away from managing work directly in [tools] 
and moving to only chatting with [Agents].

Instead of [opening the database → filtering for X → updating Y manually], 
you just tell your agent what you need. It handles the rest.

[Company] is [X]% of the way there. Their team doesn't manage work in 
[database] anymore — they manage it through conversation with agents.
```

### Example
```
Our team is starting to move 100% away from managing work themselves 
in a Notion database and moving to only chatting with Notion Agents.

Instead of opening the database → filtering for tasks → updating status manually, 
you just tell your agent what you need. It handles the rest.
```

### "From Tool User to Team Lead" Template
```
You used to be the one doing the work. Now you're the one directing it.

Instead of: open tool → find info → manually process → update records
You do: tell agent what you need → review result → done

Your role shifts from worker to team lead — directing your AI teammates 
who handle the execution.
```

## Persona-Based Value Props

Anchor agent value in how the agent adapts to YOU.

### "Learns Your Style" Template
```
[Product] learns your style, follows your instructions, and matches your vibe.

It's not a generic assistant applying the same template to everyone. 
Your agent adapts to:
- Your preferred formats and tone
- Your specific instructions and rules
- Your team's conventions and standards

The more you use it, the more it reflects how YOU work.
```

### Example
```
Personalized and private to you

Notion Agent learns your style, follows your instructions, and 
matches your vibe. It's not a generic assistant — it's YOUR agent, 
trained on how YOU work.
```

### "Your Permissions" Template
```
Every agent has its own permissions — just like a teammate.

You decide what each agent can read and write. Control access at the 
page, database, or workspace level. Audit trails on every action.

Shared infrastructure, team-wide benefits, enterprise-grade controls.
```

### Example
```
Custom Agents

Each Custom Agent gets its own permissions, just like a teammate. 
You decide what it can read. You control what it can write. 
It's team infrastructure with enterprise controls.
```

## "Works While You Sleep" Positioning

This is Notion's most powerful framing — position the product as something that handles work in your absence.

### Core Template
```
[Product] runs 24/7 — so you don't have to.

Set it up once. Then it works whether you're online or not — handling 
[recurring task], [routine update], [incoming request], while you sleep, 
while you're in meetings, while you're off.

The automation happens in the background. You see the finished result, 
not the process.

"[Specific example of what runs overnight/weekend]" — [User Quote]
```

### Example (from Notion)
```
Answers, tasks, updates — automated and running while you sleep. 
Set them up once, and the workflows run themselves.

Your agent handles the busywork. You see the finished result.
```
