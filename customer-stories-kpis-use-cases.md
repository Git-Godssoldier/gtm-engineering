### 1. Braintrust

**Tagline:** "In the age of AI, you should just refuse to do repetitive work."

**Profile:**
- AI observability platform
- Trusted by Notion, Stripe, and Replit

**The Problem (Before):**

- By 9 a.m., combed through Gong call transcripts, cross-checked Slack for deals, manually rewrote sales one-pager

- Marketing manually tracked competitor moves and customer logos
- Sales needed hours of prep to understand context before calls
- Customer support fielded same questions repeatedly

**The Solution:**
- Custom Agents handle busywork before team logs on

**The Result:**
- 20+ minutes saved per Slack update

**Quotable:**
> "In the age of AI, you should just refuse to do repetitive work."

- Support

## Storytelling Patterns Observed

### Pattern 1: "Before / After" Contrast

**Structure:**
1. Painful "before" scenario with specific examples
2. Time spent on manual work quantified
3. "After" with automated solution
4. Resulting time savings

**Example (Braintrust):**
- Before: VP does manual research 5am-9am before team logs on
- After: Custom Agents do work automatically
- Result: Hours saved, 20+ min per task

### Pattern 2: "Quote + Data" Combo

**Structure:**
1. Lead with executive quote (philosophy statement)
2. Follow with specific customer result
3. Back up with quantifiable data

**Example:**
> "In the age of AI, you should just refuse to do repetitive work." — CEO
> → Result: 20+ minutes saved per update

### Pattern 3: "Tool as Culture" Narrative

**Structure:**
1. Frame tool choice as cultural decision
2. Connect to company values
3. Show enterprise-wide adoption

**Example (Toyota, Planful):**
> "Tools shape our company culture..."
> → Company-wide enthusiastic adoption

### Pattern 4: "Scale + Efficiency" Story

**Structure:**
1. Growth challenge
2. Platform consolidation
3. Result: 5× scale with cost reduction

**Example (Parloa):**
- Grew 5×
- Cut software costs in half
- Teams focus on high-impact work

### Pattern 5: "Single Source of Truth"

**Structure:**
1. Problem: scattered information
2. Solution: unified hub
3. Result: eliminated confusion, faster workflows

**Example (OpenAI):**
- Multiple tools → single hub
- Source of truth established
- Faster workflows achieved

## Competitive Takeaways

### What Makes Notion's Stories Effective

1. **Specific time savings** — "20+ minutes," "7+ hours," not vague "improved efficiency"
2. **Executive quotes** — CEO soundbites that capture philosophy
3. **Before/after contrast** — Clear picture of transformation
4. **Company size context** — "1,600 employees" adds enterprise credibility
5. **Direct connection to business outcomes** — Shipping speed, costs, growth

### Gaps to Exploit

1. **No mention of cross-tool orchestration** — Stories focus on Notion alone
2. **Limited "agent creation" stories** — Most automate within Notion, not across tools
3. **Few "plain language" examples** — "Describe what you need" not well represented
4. **No "works while you sleep" stories** — 24/7 capability not showcased with outcomes

### Our Differentiation Opportunities

1. **Context-aware agent stories** — Use existing knowledge as context
2. **Cross-tool automation** — Not just within one tool
3. **Plain-language creation** — "Just describe what you need"
4. **24/7 autonomous operation** — Real examples of overnight/weekend work
